About Me

Hi, I’m Glory, a penultimate-year (4th-year) Mechatronics Engineering undergraduate at the Federal University of Technology, Minna. I am an independent researcher with ML Collective and a Research Intern at the IEEE Skill-Up Technology Hub at the University of Chichester. Previously, I worked as a Research Assistant in the Jossou Research Group at MIT, mentored by DongHun Ryu and supervised by Erricmore Jossou.

Research Interests

My research focuses on the design, control, and learning of autonomous systems under resource constraints. I explore parameter-efficient fine-tuning, model compression, knowledge distillation, adaptive control, and embedded machine learning to enable compute- and energy-efficient intelligent systems. My goal is to build autonomous systems that adapt robustly in complex, dynamic environments while operating on limited hardware.
